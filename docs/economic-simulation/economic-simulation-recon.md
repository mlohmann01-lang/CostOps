# economic simulation recon

## CURRENT_FORECASTING_CAPABILITIES
Deterministic spend, savings, governance drift, AI adoption, productivity, risk and confidence forecasting are available.

## CURRENT_OUTCOME_ATTRIBUTION_CAPABILITIES
Outcome attribution and duplicate suppression exist for projected vs realized value controls.

## CURRENT_GOVERNANCE_MODEL_CAPABILITIES
Governance risk engines, approval patterns, and boundary enforcement already exist and are reused.

## SCENARIO_SIMULATION_DEPENDENCIES
Economic forecasting layer, cross-domain economics, decision intelligence, outcome ledger, confidence/governance engines, replay/telemetry/lineage authorities.

## FORECAST_AND_SIMULATION_DIFFERENCES
Forecasting predicts likely trajectories; simulation compares assumption-driven alternatives and tradeoffs.

## ASSUMPTION_RISK_AREAS
Aggressive adoption, weak realization, inflation, and rollout overconfidence can distort scenario outcomes.

## DOUBLE_COUNT_RISK_AREAS
Cross-domain overlap can double count savings/productivity without duplicate suppression.

## GOVERNANCE_ESCALATION_RISKS
Shadow AI growth, bottlenecks, and ownership degradation can accelerate governance failure states.

## PRODUCTIVITY_ATTRIBUTION_LIMITATIONS
Productivity impacts remain sensitive to attribution quality, lagging signals, and cross-team confounders.

## NON_NEGOTIABLE_BOUNDARIES
Runtime modes remain READ_ONLY, RECOMMEND_ONLY, APPROVAL_REQUIRED only; no autonomous execution or subsystem forks.
