# economic forecasting recon

## CURRENT_ECONOMIC_INTELLIGENCE_CAPABILITIES
Cross-domain economics, outcome attribution, deterministic arbitration, governance risk and recommendation confidence are reused as canonical authorities.

## CURRENT_OUTCOME_ATTRIBUTION_CAPABILITIES
Outcome attribution and duplicate suppression provide replayable projected-vs-realized baselines.

## FORECASTING_DEPENDENCIES
Decision intelligence, cross-domain economics, outcome ledger, confidence engine, lineage/replay authority and runtime boundary protection.

## FORECAST_CONFIDENCE_LIMITATIONS
Sparse history, stale evidence, high duplicate-claim risk, and volatility reduce confidence aggressively.

## HISTORICAL_SIGNAL_LIMITATIONS
Early tenant rollouts may have short time-series, incomplete departmental tagging and noisy pilot cohorts.

## DUPLICATE_ATTRIBUTION_RISKS
Overlapping AI + M365 claims can inflate savings projections without strict duplicate suppression.

## GOVERNANCE_DRIFT_RISKS
Sprawl, shadow AI, approval bottlenecks, and ownership ambiguity can accelerate drift.

## AI_ADOPTION_FORECAST_RISKS
Adoption can outpace productivity realization, increasing waste and governance pressure.

## SAVINGS_REALIZATION_RISKS
Low-confidence recommendations, reversals, and repeated overrides reduce expected realization.

## NON_NEGOTIABLE_BOUNDARIES
Runtime remains READ_ONLY, RECOMMEND_ONLY, APPROVAL_REQUIRED only. No AUTO_EXECUTE, AUTO_REMEDIATE, autonomous procurement/budget/vendor actions, subsystem forks, or UI expansion.
