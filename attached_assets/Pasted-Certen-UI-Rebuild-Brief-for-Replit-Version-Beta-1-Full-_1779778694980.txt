Certen — UI Rebuild Brief for Replit
Version: Beta 1 — Full visual rebuild
Date: May 2026
Purpose: Restore and significantly upgrade the Certen platform UI. The current build has been stripped to plain text with no visual design. This document provides complete, precise instructions to rebuild every page to a world-class standard.

0. Context: What Happened and What We're Fixing
The current codebase renders all pages as raw text on a dark background — no cards, no colour coding, no visual hierarchy, no interactive components. We need to restore the richer UI that existed previously (reference screenshots attached) and improve it significantly based on the design direction below.
There are two distinct environments that must be handled differently in the UI:

Demo workspace (demo-sandbox-tenant) — synthetic data, live execution disabled. Must always show a clear amber warning banner at the top.
Live workspace — real connectors, real execution enabled. Normal UI with no demo banner.

Apply these rules to every page, every component.

15. What NOT to Do

Do not use plain <p> or <div> text dumps for structured data — always use cards, tables, or lists
Do not use SCREAMING_SNAKE_CASE labels visible to users — always humanise (e.g. "APPROVAL_REQUIRED" → "Approval required")
Do not show raw timestamps like 2026-05-01T09:00:00.000Z — format as "May 1, 2026 · 09:00" or "3h ago"
Do not show status as plain text — always use coloured pills
Do not render the lifecycle timeline as a text list — always use the visual timeline component above
Do not show a flat spend trend — the line must curve down after actions are executed
Do not use gauges, dials, or speedometer charts — ever
Do not use rainbow colours — the entire colour system is teal (primary), amber (warning), red (danger), grey (neutral)
Do not repeat "DEMO · SYNTHETIC · DEGRADED · NONE" as raw dot-separated strings — parse and render as structured pills
